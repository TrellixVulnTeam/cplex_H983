Basketball scheduling

This is an example using constraint programming to schedule the ACC basketball conference. The original model is described by G.L. Nemhauser and M.A. Trick, Scheduling a Major College Basketball Conference, Operations Research, 46: 1-8 (1998). Martin Henz created an alternate formulation using constraint programming in M. Henz, Scheduling a Major College Basketball Conference - Revisited, Operations Research, 49: 163-168 (2001). The example has been adapted for OPL.

Note: This example is not supported if you are running CPLEX Studio Preview Edition.

The characteristics of the basketball scheduling example are the following:

  • Industry: Time tabling
  • Feature: OPL Project
  • Technique: Constraint Programming
  • Complexity: Intermediate
  • Keywords: Scheduling, Team assignment, Sports
  • Works with the Preview Edition: No
  • Project Location: examples/opl/BasketballScheduling
  • Model Name: basketballscheduling.mod

Manpower planning

This model from Model Building in Mathematical Programming, by H.P. Williams, finds a plan for recruiting, training, staffing, overtime, and redundancy declaration, in order to meet the staffing requirements for a facility over the next years. The objective is to minimize redundancy, but also reduce costs. This model assumes that all staffing changes are taken into account on the first of the year.

The characteristics of the manpower planning example are the following:

  • Industry: Time tabling
  • Feature: OPL Project
  • Technique: Linear Programming
  • Complexity: Basic
  • Keywords: Staffing, Staff Planning
  • Project Location: examples/opl/models/Manpower
  • Model Name: manpower.mod

Staff assignment

A fast-food franchise runs from 8 a.m. to 8 p.m. on Monday to Friday. Each working day is divided into three 4-hour shifts:

  • Breakfast: 8 a.m. - 12 noon

  • Lunch: 12 noon - 4 p.m.

  • Dinner: 4 p.m. - 8 p.m.

Each shift demands three types of personnel: cook, cashier, and cleaner. Each of its personnel is capable of performing a subset of these tasks. Some people may not be available on certain days. If scheduled, each person works 8 hours a day (2 shifts in this case), and prefers the shifts to be consecutive. Also, if the person works on a night shift, he or she prefers not to work on the morning shift the next day.

The objective is to find a balanced assignment of workers to shifts that minimizes the total number of unfilled slots.

The characteristics of the staff assignment example are the following:

  • Industry: Time tabling
  • Feature: OPL Project
  • Technique: Integer Programming
  • Complexity: Intermediate
  • Keywords: Staffing, Staff assignment, retail, fast food
  • Project Location: examples/opl/models/Staffing
  • Model Name: staffing.mod

See also the nurse scheduling problem, Relaxing infeasible models, in the IDE User’s Manual.

Task scheduling

This example involves assigning dates and workers to tasks. The model takes a list of service requests, and based on their request type, creates the necessary tasks and dependencies. The objective is to assign dates and resources and meet the due dates within the slack provided by the user.

The characteristics of the task scheduling example are the following:

  • Industry: Time tabling
  • Feature: OPL Project
  • Technique: CP Scheduling
  • Complexity: Advanced
  • Keywords: Scheduling, worker assignment
  • Project Location: examples/opl/models/sched_tasks
  • Model Name: sched_tasks.mod
